Use Case Scenarios

Scenario 1: Regular Drowsy Driver

Driver begins to show signs of drowsiness but responds to initial alerts and recovers safely.

T0
12% Drowsiness - Normal Driving Vehicle operating normally, all systems green
T5
45% Drowsiness - Stage 1 Alert Subtle seat vibration detected, driver receives gentle warning
T10
18% Drowsiness - Driver Responds Driver opens eyes, increases alertness, score drops back to normal
Result: Safe recovery. Vehicle continues normally. No autonomous stop required.

Scenario 2: Driver Ignores All Alerts

Drowsiness escalates through all stages because driver doesn't respond to warnings.

T0
45% - Stage 1 (Vibration) Gentle seat vibration. Driver ignores.
T3
62% - Stage 2 (Sound + Haptic) Dog bark or voice alert + stronger vibration. Driver ignores.
T8
78% - Stage 3 (Critical) Red dashboard, urgent voice "VEHICLE STOPPING IN 5 SECONDS". External beacon flashing red.
T15
92% - Autonomous Stop Engaged Vehicle brakes automatically. Hazard lights on. Beacon strobing. Public warned.
Result: Emergency intervention. Vehicle stopped. Driver must manually resume. System protects all road users.

Scenario 3: Post-Seizure Medical Episode

System detects drowsiness from medical recovery (seizure aftermath) and adapts response with dignity.

T0
Medical Flag: Seizure Recovery Detected System recognizes unusual eye movement pattern consistent with post-seizure state
T3
55% Drowsiness - Gentle Alert Path System skips aggressive stages. Message: "Your seizure recovery was detected. We're protecting you."
T5
Vehicle Stops Safely Gentle deceleration to shoulder. Screen offers: Medical resources, Emergency contact, Doctor notification
Result: Medical-aware intervention. Driver supported, not punished. Public safety maintained with dignity.

Scenario 4: Pedestrian Perspective

Pedestrian approaching intersection receives safety information through external beacon.

T0
Vehicle Approaching (Normal) No beacon active. Pedestrian assumes driver is alert. Prepares to cross.
T3
Beacon Pulses Amber (Stage 1) Pedestrian notices subtle pulsing. Intuitive reaction: "Something's off. I'll wait."
T8
Beacon Flashing Faster (Stage 2) Pedestrian now certain: "Driver is compromised. Definitely not crossing."
T15
Vehicle Stops, Beacon Red Strobe Pedestrian safely crosses. Crisis averted. System protected everyone.
Result: Public safety through transparency. Pedestrian made informed decision. Collision prevented.

About This System

Design Philosophy

This system is built on three core principles:

  • Safety Override Hierarchy: Some warnings can be dismissed (speed limit). Drowsiness alerts cannot. External beacon cannot be disabled.
  • Medical Dignity: System respects medical conditions (seizure recovery, hypoglycemia, cardiac arrhythmia) while maintaining public safety.
  • Transparent Public Safety: Pedestrians and other drivers deserve to know vehicle status. External beacon provides this information.

Not Traditional ADAS

This is a Driver State Monitoring System, not traditional Advanced Driver Assistance (ADAS). It monitors the human, not the vehicle. When driving becomes unsafe due to driver state, it intervenes autonomously.

Design Scope vs. Engineering Scope

Design scope (what's in this prototype):
  • UX/interaction design for driver alerts
  • Human-centered problem framing
  • Escalation logic and feedback systems
  • Safety principles and ethics

Engineering scope (requires partners):
  • Sensor specifications (eye-tracking accuracy, latency)
  • Medical validation (drowsiness thresholds, edge cases)
  • Hardware engineering (beacon placement, circuits)
  • Regulatory compliance (NHTSA, EU standards)
  • Production feasibility (cost, miniaturization, power)
