The Industry Offers
Optional
Removable glass. Replaceable covers. Sophisticated hoods with optional protection. Every version is a negotiation with safety.
What That Actually Means
Compromised
When safety is optional — someone will choose not to take it. A cracked display ignored. A glass removed for looks. A rider making the wrong call.
What This Concept Demands
Permanent
Structural. Engineered to the life of the vehicle. Not serviceable by the rider. Not a decision. Not a choice. Just always there. Like an engine.
The Difference

Compromised
vs Permanent.

Safety Element Current Industry Standard Concept 11 — Permanent Standard
Display Glass Replaceable. Consumer serviceable. Optional upgrade. Structural. Authorised service only. Life of vehicle.
Mirror Mount Removable bolt-on. Rider removes at will. No consequence. Integrated into fairing or handlebar. Non-removable by design.
Hood Protection Aesthetic cover. Protects display from style, not impact. Structural canopy. Rated protection. Permanent shade + shield.
Rider Decision Required. Rider chooses safety level. Most choose convenience. Eliminated. Safety is the default. No decision needed.
Design Philosophy Safety as optional feature. Premium upgrade. Safety as structure. Non-negotiable foundation.
Analogy Optional seatbelt in a car. The brake system. Always there. No version without it.
